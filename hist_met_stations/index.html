Historical Meteorological Stations in The Pannonian Basin (1871-1919)

The Royal Hungarian Central Institute of Meteorology and Earth Magnetism was founded in 1870, and its yearbooks were published between 1871-1919 and 1939-1944. The original paper-format yearbooks and their scanned versions are availabe in the ELTE University Library of Budapest, Hungary, containing detailed daily records and monthly averages of temperature, humidity, precipitation, air pressure, wind direction and other measurements. Hundreds of stations were active in this period (peak year was 1911 with more than 1400 active stations), yet this dataset has barely been analized due to its analog data format.

The map below aims to visualize the territorial coverage of the stations, taking into account that some of them remained active throughout the whole period, while others were functioning only for a couple of years. For an overview of the stations and their activity, choose the „Overview” map to find stations which have extensive data series. Alternatively, feel free to explore how the activity of the stations changed over time by scrolling through the years, focusing on temperature or precipitation measuring locations.
